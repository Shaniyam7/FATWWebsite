잡채 : Stir-fried Glass Noodles

Being one of the most beloved dishes in South Korea, 잡채 consists of stir-fried potato starch noodles with vegetables and tender meat!

Ingredients

  • 7 ounces Korean potato starch noodles
  • 1 carrot
  • 1/2 medium onion
  • 2 scallions
  • 4 ounces lean tender beef
  • 3-4 ounces fresh shiitake mushrooms
  • 6 ounces fresh spinach
  • 1 egg garnish (optional)
  • Oil
  • Salt

Sauce

  • 3 1/2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 3 tablespoons sugar
  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil
  • 2 teaspoons minced garlic
  • 1 tablespoon roasted sesame seeds
  • Black pepper (to taste)

Recipe Instructions

  1. Clean all the ingredients. Cut the carrot into matchsticks, thinly slice the onion, prepare the scallions, and remove the stems from the mushrooms.
  2. Combine all the sauce ingredients in a small bowl and mix well until the sugar is dissolved.
  3. In a large non-stick pan, bring about 5 cups of water to a boil, and cook the noodles until completely translucent and soft (6 to 8 minutes). Drain in a strainer and rinse under cold running water. Roughly cut the noodles with a pair of kitchen shears or a knife into 6-7 inch lengths.
  4. Fill the same pan with 2 cups of water. Blanch the spinach only until wilted. Drain quickly and shock in cold water. Squeeze out the water and chop the spinach. Lightly season with salt.
  5. Add 1/2 tablespoon of oil to the pan, and stir fry the noodles over medium to medium-high heat, stirring frequently, until translucent and a bit sticky (about 4 minutes). Transfer to a large mixing bowl.
  6. Add 1/2 tablespoon of oil to the pan, and stir fry the carrot over medium-high heat. When the carrot is halfway cooked, push it to one side of the pan and add the onion slices. Stir-fry until translucent. Add the scallions and cook briefly. Transfer to the mixing bowl with the noodles.
  7. Add the beef and mushrooms to the pan with 2 tablespoons of the prepared sauce and cook until the meat is cooked through, 2-3 minutes. Transfer to the mixing bowl with the noodles and vegetables.
  8. Add the spinach and the remaining sauce to the bowl with all other prepared ingredients. Toss well by hand. Adjust the seasoning with additional soy sauce and/or sugar if needed.
  9. Optional garnish: Separate the egg white and yolk. Lightly beat the white and yolk separately. Heat a lightly oiled nonstick skillet over medium-low heat. Pour each egg part into a thin layer. Cook each side briefly without browning. Slice the cooked eggs into thin strips and use as a garnish.
